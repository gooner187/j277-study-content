```
keywords: open source, benefits to users, source code, edit, adapt, free, redistribute, fix bugs, open source licence
year: 2023 | paper: J277 | question: 5d(ii) | marks: 2
```

**Question:**  
Describe one benefit to the users of releasing the software as open source.

**Mark Scheme** (1 mark for point + 1 mark for expansion):

- Users can view/edit the source code / users can edit the program/software
  - → to tailor/improve/adapt it to do what they need/want

## Proprietary Software – Benefits to Developer

### Q: Describe two benefits to the developer of releasing software as proprietary [4 marks]

```
keywords: proprietary software, benefits to developer, earn money, licence, source code protected, intellectual property, control, proprietary licence
year: 2023 | paper: J277 | question: 5d(i) | marks: 4
```

**Question:**  
Describe two benefits to the artist and programmer of releasing the software as proprietary.

**Mark Scheme** (1 mark for point + 1 mark for expansion, max 4):

- Authors can earn money
  - → by selling for a fee / using licences to stop unauthorised use
- No-one can see the code
  - → users cannot edit/change the software / cannot insert malware
  - → so it cannot be copied/resold/shared
- More control over intellectual property
  - → restrict users / restrict what can be done with the software without permission

**Examiner Guidance:**

- Benefit must be to the artist and programmer — NOT to the user
- Do NOT award reference to ownership/copyright because both licence types allow copyright of the code

## Open Source vs Proprietary – Extended Discussion (QER)

### Q: Discuss open source vs proprietary software for a downloadable game [8 marks]

```
keywords: open source, proprietary software, features, benefits, drawbacks, legal issues, ethical issues, licence, recommendation, extended response, QER
year: 2024 | paper: J277 | question: 4* | marks: 8
```

**Question:**  
A computer programmer has developed a computer game that they want to release for users to download over the internet. The programmer needs to decide whether to release the game as open source or proprietary software.  
Discuss the features, benefits and drawbacks of each type of licence for this program and make a recommendation to the programmer.  
You should include: features of each licence; legal and ethical issues of each licence; benefits and drawbacks of each licence.

**Mark Scheme (Levels of Response, max 8 marks):**

**Mark Band 3 – High Level (6–8 marks):**  
Thorough knowledge and understanding. Applied directly and consistently to context. Weighs up both sides and references impact on all areas. Well-developed, clear, logically structured. Covers ALL required elements (legal/ethical, benefits, drawbacks for both types) with a clear, structured recommendation.

**Mark Band 2 – Mid Level (3–5 marks):**  
Reasonable knowledge; generally accurate but at times underdeveloped. Applied to context, though opportunities missed. Includes one or more from legal/ethical, benefits, drawbacks for open source OR proprietary, OR a justified recommendation without clearly referencing all bullet points.

**Mark Band 1 – Low Level (1–2 marks):**  
Basic knowledge; limited understanding; some inaccuracies. Limited attempt to apply knowledge. Unsupported assertion; unstructured. Limited to facts about open source and/or proprietary only.

**0 marks:** No attempt, or not worthy of credit.

**Indicative Content:**

*Licence Features:*
- Open source – usually free; can access/change source code; can redistribute
- Proprietary – purchased at a cost; cannot access/change source code

*Legal and Ethical Issues:*
- Both types provide copyright protection
- Open source – allows others to take and possibly change the code to resell, adapt, or claim as their own (reverse for proprietary)
- Open source – allows more people access because there is likely no cost (reverse for proprietary)

*Benefits and Drawbacks:*
- Open source – wider customer base; more exposure; users can fix bugs; limited documentation; little financial gain
- Proprietary – allows programmer to earn money; more control; usually well tested; more copyright restrictions; cannot be adapted to meet user needs

*Recommendation:*
- Either type is appropriate; justification must be clearly for this scenario (a downloadable game)

**Examiner Guidance:**

- AO2 1a (4 marks) + AO2 1b (4 marks)
- Annotate with L1, L2, or L3 only

## Creative Commons Licence

### Q: Explain how a Creative Commons licence will impact the use of someone's videos [3 marks]

```
keywords: Creative Commons licence, copyright, free distribution, attribution, no derivatives, non-commercial, redistribute, edit
year: 2018 | paper: J276 | question: 1d | marks: 3
```

**Question:**  
William wants to upload his videos on the Internet and is considering releasing them under a Creative Commons licence.  
Explain how a Creative Commons licence will impact the use of William's videos by other people.

**Mark Scheme** (1 mark per bullet, max 3):

- Allows free distribution / other people can use/edit his work
- Other people can redistribute his work
- Can choose to restrict other people from being able to use/edit/share the videos
- Work is still copyrighted / others cannot claim it as their own
- No-derivative option: William can set that if others edit it they cannot redistribute it with the edits
- Attribution option: William can insist on having his name on it if re-used / must be credited
- Can insist on non-commercial use / others cannot sell/profit from his work / personal use only

**Examiner Guidance:**

- "People need to ask to use it" is NOT enough

## Environmental Impacts of Technology

### Q: Describe the environmental impacts of bringing out new devices twice a year [2 marks]

```
keywords: environmental impact, e-waste, pollution, natural resources, distribution, carbon footprint, new devices, planned obsolescence
year: 2021 | paper: J276 | question: 3a | marks: 2
```

**Question:**  
A technology company brings out new, updated devices twice a year.  
Describe the environmental impacts of the company bringing out new devices twice a year.

**Mark Scheme** (1 mark each, max 2):

- Old devices may just be thrown away → increasing pollution / e-waste that will not degrade
- New equipment uses up natural resources
- New equipment increases distribution/transport pollution
- New devices may run more efficiently (positive impact)
- Other people can use the old ones second-hand → instead of more resources being used

---

### Q: Describe the environmental impact of selling software online only [2 marks]

```
keywords: environmental impact, online distribution, no physical copies, plastic, packaging, carbon footprint, manufacturing, distribution, digital download
year: 2019 | paper: J276 | question: 6a | marks: 2
```

**Question:**  
Fiona is considering selling the game online and not making it available physically in shops.  
Describe the environmental impact of Fiona's decision.

**Mark Scheme** (1 mark per bullet, max 2):

*Benefits of not providing physical copies:*
- Less/no plastic/paper/raw materials used in manufacture / no need for packaging / less waste
- Less electrical power needed to manufacture
- No petrol used to distribute/collect → smaller carbon footprint
- Fewer discs need to be manufactured
- Fewer factory emissions / less pollution
- Old versions will not be thrown away

**Examiner Guidance:**

- Could be read as still physically creating but not putting in shops
- Do NOT accept "more use of computers/electricity to download"

**Question:**  
The use of digital technology in medicine is constantly evolving.

**Mark Scheme (Levels of Response, max 8 marks):**

**Mark Band 3 – High Level (6–8 marks):**  
Thorough knowledge and understanding across a wide range of considerations. Applied directly and consistently to context. Weighs up both sides; covers all three areas (diagnosis, treatment, storage). Well-developed, clear, logically structured reasoning. Relevant and substantiated.

## Extended Response – BYOD in Schools

```
keywords: BYOD, bring your own device, ethical issues, legal issues, privacy issues, school network, monitoring, digital divide, data protection, extended response, QER

```

**Question:**  
A school asks its students to bring in their own electronic devices (e.g. tablets, laptops, mobile phones) to use in lessons instead of purchasing new equipment.  
Discuss the issues surrounding students bringing their own devices. Include in your discussion: ethical issues; legal issues; privacy issues.

**Mark Scheme (Levels of Response, max 8 marks):**

**Mark Band 3 – High Level (6–8 marks):**  
Thorough knowledge and understanding. Applied directly and consistently to context. Weighs up both sides of the discussion; references all three areas (ethical, legal, privacy). Well-developed, clear, logically structured reasoning. Covers ethical, legal AND privacy; must include BOTH positive AND negative aspects.

**Mark Band 2 – Mid Level (3–5 marks):**  
Reasonable knowledge; generally accurate. Applied to context, though opportunities missed. Covers at least 2 of the three areas; may be only positive OR negative.

**Mark Band 1 – Low Level (1–2 marks):**  
Basic knowledge; limited understanding. Limited attempt to apply to context. Covers only one of the three areas.

**0 marks:** No attempt, or not worthy of credit.

**Indicative Content:**

*Ethical Issues:*
- Pressure on students to have the best / most up-to-date devices
- Some students may not have devices → inequality / not on a level playing field

- Responsibility for damage to devices on school premises
- Personal devices may not have appropriate child safety restrictions in place
- School may not be able to enforce acceptable use policies on personal devices

- Students may have their internet traffic monitored while on the school network
- School may wish to install monitoring software on students' personal devices
- Students may use cameras/microphones on their devices to record other students or staff

- AO2 1a (4 marks) + AO2 1b (4 marks)

Social networking websites use artificial intelligence (AI) to monitor posts from users.

- The process is repeated
- The program counter is incremented
